      6. Polynomials are a special type of mathematical function, or process for turning one number into another. Polynomials are functions of the form \(f(x)=a_{n}x^{n}+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\dots+a_{1}x^{1}+a_{0}\text{:}\) sums of powers of \(x\text{,}\) maybe with constant terms in front. For example, \(f(x)=x+1\text{,}\) \(g(x)=17x^{3}-234x+7\text{,}\) and \(h(x)=-x^{1}42+1\) are all polynomials. The polynomial \(a(x)=x^{2}+2x+3\) takes the input 1 and turns it into the output \(1^{2}+2(1)+3=6\text{.}\) All numbers which make sense to input are called the domain of the function, and the possible sensical outputs you could get are called the range.
